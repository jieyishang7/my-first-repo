Mapping My Weekly Rhythm

Digital Object 2: Temporal Structures

This project maps a typical week as a temporal structure. Class meetings, commuting, meals, walking, museums, homework, exercise, entertainment time, and sleep are translated into visual data to show how a weekly rhythm is organized across repeated days.

Temporal Structure Description

Beyond visualization, this temporal structure serves as a functional tool for my future projects. It enables me to optimize my time allocation by tracking the duration of specific tasks during different phases of a project, such as research, development, and testing. This approach helps identify bottlenecks and improves my workflow efficiency by providing a clear, measurable overview of how time is distributed across project milestones.

Weekly Schedule Gantt Chart

This chart translates the week into horizontal time blocks. It shows how classes, homework, commuting, food, walking, exercise, museums, shopping, entertainment time, and sleep occupy different parts of each day.

Time Category Summary

This summary aggregates the schedule into total weekly hours by category, making the balance between study, sleep, movement, culture, care, and entertainment time easier to compare.

Daily Energy Heatmap

This heatmap reads the same week as a field of energy and intensity. Darker cells indicate periods of higher focus, movement, or pressure, while lighter cells show rest, transition, or lower-intensity time.
